- **CiPD Associate System Designer (75/100):** Strong overlap — embedded
  product development, microcontroller interfacing, C/C++, PCB design
  knowledge desirable. Main blocker: requires 2 years post-grad experience.
- **Champion Semiconductor Hardware Intern (55/100):** Aligns with target
  industry (semiconductor), but posting is vague and stale (4 months old).
- **CiPD Senior System Designer (50/100):** Same scope as Associate role but
  higher experience bar due to "Senior" framing.
- **Hypertangent Mechatronics (40/100):** Adjacent but diverges toward
  mechatronics rather than PCB/firmware focus.

---

## 🧩 Skill-Gap Analysis

**Most commonly required skills across postings:**
- C/C++ programming
- Microcontroller knowledge and interfacing
- Hands-on electronics/hardware development
- Embedded product development experience
- PCB/schematic design (desirable)
- Testing and documentation

**Gap identified:**
- My STM32, FreeRTOS, KiCad, and sensor-interfacing skills match well
- Most postings (especially CiPD) require **2+ years post-graduation
  experience** — a hard filter for a second-year student
- PCB design is "desirable," not mandatory — so my EAGLE/KiCad work is a
  plus, not a gating factor
- Target companies (STMicroelectronics, NXP, Bosch, TI, DRDO, Tata Elxsi,
  Marvell) **did not appear** in Indeed listings at all

---

## 📈 Market Demand Insights

- Embedded systems internships in Delhi/NCR are **sparse on Indeed**
- Most listings are either fresher-level full-time roles or vague
  "hardware/electronics" postings from smaller companies
- Large semiconductor/automotive firms likely post through **their own
  career portals or campus placement cells** rather than Indeed

---

## ✅ Recommendations to Improve Interview Chances

1. **Apply directly through target companies' career portals**
   (STMicroelectronics, NXP, Bosch, TI) — these rarely surface on Indeed
2. **Reach out directly to CiPD/IIIT Delhi** even without 2 years experience
   — strong project portfolio (STM32 capstone, KiCad PCBs) may offset this
3. **Make GitHub repos and certifications live** before applying — recruiters
   for embedded roles check code quality
4. **Leverage IIT Madras alumni network and campus placement cell** for
   direct pipelines to DRDO, Tata Elxsi, etc.
5. **Complete the STM32 capstone (air quality monitor)** before applying —
   directly addresses the "one project in Embedded domain" requirement

---

## 🎯 Overall Fit Assessment

My skill set (Embedded C, STM32, PCB design, sensor interfacing) aligns well
with the *type* of work in these postings. However, Delhi/NCR Indeed
listings are thin and skew toward post-graduate experience levels rather
than student internships. The main constraint isn't fit — it's that target
companies and roles aren't well-represented on job boards. **Direct outreach
and campus channels will likely yield better-matched opportunities** than
continued job board searches.

---

## 💡 Key Learnings

**1. AI-powered job search needs detailed profile context**
Giving Claude my full skill set, target companies, and constraints allowed
it to generate match scores instead of generic listings.

**2. Match scores reveal real gaps, not just keyword matches**
A 75/100 match still flagged a hard experience requirement — something a
simple keyword search wouldn't catch.

**3. Job boards aren't the full picture**
Top target companies didn't appear on Indeed at all — campus placement
cells and direct career portals matter more for large firms.

**4. Skill gaps can be addressed proactively**
Instead of just identifying gaps, Claude suggested specific actions
(finish the STM32 capstone, push GitHub repos live) to close them before
applying.

**5. Personal projects are leverage, not just resume filler**
My existing projects (STM32 roadmap, PCB designs, dashboards) were
specifically cited as ways to offset the experience gap.
